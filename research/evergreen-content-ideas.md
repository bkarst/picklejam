# 100 Evergreen Content Ideas — Ranked by SEO Benefit to Pickle Jam

> Derived from `research/seo-keyword-research.md` (Google Keyword Planner, US, Jun 2025–May 2026). Every idea targets keywords from that analysis (Categories 1–5). Titles are written as engaging, publish-ready headlines that still carry the target keyword. The list below is a single flat ranking, **ordered by how much each piece benefits Pickle Jam's SEO** — not by category and not by business value. 🔥 = +900% YoY growth.

## How SEO benefit was scored

1. **Traffic ceiling** — total search volume of the keyword cluster the piece can capture, not just its head term.
2. **Ranking feasibility** — Low competition beats High at equal volume; a page that can't realistically rank delivers no SEO benefit.
3. **Long-tail & cluster capture** — programmatic templates (one build → hundreds of indexed pages) and pillar hubs (one page → dozens of long-tail rankings) score far above single-keyword articles.
4. **Link & authority earning** — free tools, printables, calculators, and citable reference content attract backlinks that lift the *entire domain's* rankings, a multiplier no single article has.
5. **Durability** — fully evergreen beats semi-evergreen (player profiles and streaming guides need refreshes and are slightly discounted).

**Note — SEO benefit ≠ business value.** This ordering deliberately differs from a revenue ranking: the high-CPC organizer terms (`pickleball league software`, `pickleball ladder`, bids to $12.50) fall far down this list because almost nobody searches them — they're conversion plays, not SEO plays. Conversely, pro-player profiles and the glossary climb because they're 10K–100K/Low-competition ranking wins, even though they convert weakly.

**Note — cannibalization control.** Near-duplicate search intents are deliberately merged onto single URLs (court dimensions + court size, scoring + scorekeeping, seniors guide + senior benefits, elbow + elbow paddle weight, the racquet comparisons, the beginner-rules listicles) — splitting one intent across two pages weakens both. The freed slots went to keyword-backed brand, venue, and head-term opportunities: Selkirk, JOOLA, Pickleball Kingdom/indoor chains, pickleball bars, the best-paddles head roundup, and custom paddles.

## Publishing notes

- **Sequence:** work the list top-down — the top 12 are where volume, low competition, and compounding structures (programmatic templates, pillars, tools) coincide. Among equals, ship the 🔥 +900% YoY items (court size in feet, pickleball on a tennis court, tournaments near me, Selkirk, tournament registration, rules-same-as-tennis) while they're still uncontested.
- **Fun title = H1, keyword-first = `<title>` tag.** Several headlines lead with the hook and push the keyword past Google's ~60-character title truncation. Ship both versions: the fun headline as the on-page H1, and a keyword-first variant as the SEO title tag (e.g. H1 "From Backyard Boredom to National Obsession: The History of Pickleball" → title tag "History of Pickleball: From Backyard Boredom to National Obsession").
- **Structure:** build the pillar pages (court dimensions, how to play, rules, scoring, strategy, drills, beginner hub, best-paddles roundup) as hubs and internally link every supporting article up to its pillar — that topical-authority structure is how Low-competition content outranks incumbents.
- **One intent, one URL:** the merged entries (#3, #39, #64, #66, #67, #86) exist because splitting a single search intent across two pages cannibalizes both. Apply the same test to any future addition before publishing it as a separate page.
- **Don't skip the bottom for business reasons:** the organizer pieces ranked 70–90 (run-a-tournament, start-a-league, formats, league software, ladders) are weak *SEO* plays but the strongest *revenue* plays in the research — schedule them on conversion value, not this ranking.
- **Every article funnels:** end each piece with the court finder, event finder, or free organizer tools. The content is the moat; the directory and SaaS are the business.
